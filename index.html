Our Story

In the rural communities of the Philippines, rice farming is the lifeblood that sustains families for generations. With each harvest, hope for a better future is rekindled, but the harsh reality of their daily struggle leaves them drowning in debt and despair.

The very chemicals that they rely on to yield a fruitful crop are poisoning their land and endangering their health. The cost of these toxic substances is a heavy burden that they cannot bear alone.

At Bagor Tech, we believe that the cycle of hopelessness that rice farmers in the Philippines face is not the end of their story. We know that these hardworking individuals deserve a chance to break free from poverty and debt, and to provide for their families with dignity. As a team, we understand the struggles that these farmers endure, and the impact it has on entire communities. That's why we have made it our mission to provide them with the knowledge, tools, and support to transition to sustainable and natural farming methods. Together, we can create a brighter future for all.

Answer: The SRI method is a more sustainable way of growing rice that focuses on enhancing soil health, reducing water use, and improving yields. It involves using fewer seeds, wider spacing between plants, transplanting younger seedlings, and using natural growth stimulants to improve plant growth and soil fertility.

Answer: The SRI method can lead to lower input costs, higher yields, and a reduction in the use of synthetic chemicals that can harm the environment and human health. It also enhances soil health, reduces water use, and improves crop resilience to climate change.

Answer: The excessive use of synthetic chemicals can cause respiratory problems, skin irritation, and other health issues for farmers and their families. The chemicals can also contaminate water sources, harm non-target organisms, and lead to soil degradation.

Answer: Natural growth stimulants such as compost, manure, and organic matter can enhance soil fertility, improve plant growth, and reduce the need for synthetic chemicals. They can also reduce input costs and enhance the sustainability of rice farming.
